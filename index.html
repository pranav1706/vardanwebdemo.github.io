TMT Bar

Download Brochure

  • TMT bars, or Thermo-Mechanically Treated bars, are widely used in construction to reinforce concrete structures. These bars, made of low carbon steel, possess high tensile strength and are engineered for durability and stability.

  • By incorporating elements such as manganese, chromium, and vanadium, their strength and longevity are further enhanced. Stringent quality testing ensures their reliability.

  • TMT bars are available in various sizes, ranging from 6mm to 40mm in diameter and 12m to 15m in length.

  • It is important to note that the composition of TMT bars can vary depending on the desired grade and properties, necessitating reference to the manufacturer's specifications for precise information.

Fe415

This grade of TMT bar has a minimum yield strength of 415 N/mm². It is commonly used in residential and small-scale construction projects.

Fe500

This grade of TMT bar has a minimum yield strength of 500 N/mm². It is widely used in various construction projects, including buildings, bridges, and infrastructure.

Fe500D

The "D" in Fe500D stands for "ductility." This grade of TMT bar has enhanced ductility, which means it can undergo greater deformation before failure. Fe500D bars are suitable for earthquake-prone areas and structures where increased flexibility is required.

Fe550

This grade of TMT bar has a minimum yield strength of 550 N/mm². It is used in heavy-duty construction projects that require higher strength and load-bearing capacity.

Fe550D

Similar to Fe500D, Fe550D TMT bars also offer enhanced ductility. These bars are suitable for structures subjected to dynamic and seismic loads.

Fe600

This grade of TMT bar has a minimum yield strength of 600 N/mm². Fe600 bars are used in specialized applications where extremely high strength is required, such as in industrial structures or high-rise buildings.

Cement

Download Brochure

Cement is a binding material used in construction that is primarily responsible for holding various components of a structure together. It is a fine powder made from a mixture of materials, including limestone, clay, shells, and silica sand. The process of manufacturing cement involves crushing, grinding, heating, and blending these raw materials to form a powdered substance.

OPC-53 Grade Cement

OPC-53 Grade Cement is a type of Ordinary Portland Cement with a compressive strength of 53 MPa (MegaPascals). It is known for its high strength and is suitable for construction projects that require strong and durable structures. OPC-53 Grade Cement is commonly used in the construction of bridges, high-rise buildings, dams, and other heavy-duty structures.

OPC-43 Grade Cement

OPC-43 Grade Cement is another type of Ordinary Portland Cement with a compressive strength of 43 MPa. It is widely used in general construction works where high strength is not the primary requirement. OPC-43 Grade Cement is suitable for applications such as residential buildings, commercial complexes, and small-scale infrastructure projects.

PPC Cement

PPC (Portland Pozzolana Cement) is a type of blended cement that contains pozzolanic materials such as fly ash, volcanic ash, or silica fumes along with Ordinary Portland Cement. The addition of pozzolanic materials enhances the workability, durability, and sustainability of the cement. PPC Cement is commonly used in various construction applications, including residential, commercial, and infrastructure projects.

Steel Structure

Structural steel is a category of steel used for making construction materials in a variety of shapes. They are designed, fabricated, and erected using engineering principles and specialized techniques. Steel structures offer advantages such as high strength, durability, flexibility, and speed of construction. In modern construction, steel structures is used for almost every type of structure including heavy industrial building, high-rise building, equipment support system, infrastructure, bridge, tower, airport terminal, heavy industrial plant, pipe rack, etc.

MS Angle

MS Angle, also known as Mild Steel Angle, is a structural steel shape with two legs of equal length that are joined at a 90-degree angle. It is commonly used in construction projects to provide support and stability. MS Angles are available in various sizes and thicknesses.

MS Flat Bar

MS Flat Bar is a long, flat piece of mild steel with rectangular cross-sections. It is widely used in the construction industry for making frames, brackets, and other structural components. MS Flat Bars come in different widths, thicknesses, and lengths.

MS Round Bar

MS Round Bar, also known as Mild Steel Round Bar, is a cylindrical-shaped solid steel bar. It is commonly used in construction, manufacturing, and fabrication applications. MS Round Bars are available in various diameters and lengths.

MS Square Bar

MS Square Bar is a square-shaped solid steel bar made from mild steel. It is often used for making shafts, supports, and other structural components. MS Square Bars come in different sizes and lengths.

MS Channel

MS Channel, also known as Mild Steel Channel, is a "C"-shaped structural steel section with a wide range of applications. It is commonly used in the construction of frames, supports, and bracing elements. MS Channels are available in different sizes and thicknesses.

I-Beam

I-Beam, also known as H-Beam or Universal Beam, is a long steel beam with an I or H-shaped cross-section. It is widely used in construction projects for providing structural support and load-bearing capabilities. I-Beams come in various sizes and lengths.

MS Pipe

MS Pipe, also known as Mild Steel Pipe, is a hollow cylindrical tube made from mild steel. It is commonly used for conveying fluids, gases, and other substances in various industries. MS Pipes come in different sizes, thicknesses, and lengths.

GI pipe

GI Pipe, also known as Galvanized Iron pipe, is a coated pipe that has a layer of zinc to prevent corrosion. It is commonly used in plumbing, water supply, and construction for its durability and resistance to rust. Regular maintenance is necessary to ensure its longevity.

Paints & Finishes

Paints and finishes play a crucial role in enhancing the aesthetic appeal and protecting the surfaces of buildings. They are available in a wide range of colors, types, and finishes, each designed for specific applications and environments. Let's explore the different types of paints and finishes commonly used in buildings:

Interior Paints

  • Latex Paint: Also known as water-based paint, latex paint is the most popular choice for interior walls and ceilings. It dries quickly, has low odor, and is easy to clean up with water.

  • Oil-Based Paint: Oil-based paints provide a durable and smooth finish. They are commonly used on trim, doors, and furniture. However, they have a strong odor and require solvents for cleanup.

Exterior Paints

  • Acrylic Paint: Acrylic paint is a popular choice for exterior surfaces due to its durability, flexibility, and resistance to fading. It can withstand various weather conditions and is easy to clean.

  • Elastomeric Paint: Elastomeric paint is a thick, flexible coating that can bridge cracks and withstand extreme weather conditions. It provides excellent protection for exterior masonry surfaces.

Specialized Paints

  • Primer: Primers are used as a preparatory coat before applying the final paint. They improve adhesion, promote uniform color, and enhance the durability of the topcoat.

  • Anti-Mold Paint: his type of paint contains additives that inhibit the growth of mold and mildew, making it suitable for areas prone to moisture, such as bathrooms and basements.

Finishes

  • Matte Finish: Matte finishes have a non-reflective, smooth appearance and are ideal for hiding surface imperfections. They are commonly used in living rooms, bedrooms, and ceilings.

  • Satin Finish: Satin finishes offer a subtle sheen and are more durable than matte finishes. They are often used in high-traffic areas, such as hallways, kitchens, and bathrooms.

  • Glossy Finish: Glossy finishes provide a shiny, reflective surface and are highly durable and easy to clean. They are commonly used on doors, trims, and furniture.

When selecting paints and finishes for buildings, consider factors such as the surface type, location (interior or exterior), desired durability, and environmental conditions. It's important to follow the manufacturer's guidelines for proper application and maintenance to ensure long-lasting results.

Electrical Products

We offer a wide range of electrical products for buildings. Whether you are an architect, contractor, or homeowner, this comprehensive guide will provide you with essential information about electrical products used in building construction and maintenance. From wiring devices to lighting fixtures, we cover a wide range of products that ensure safety, functionality, and energy efficiency in your building projects.

Wiring and cables

Electrical wiring is essential for transmitting electricity throughout a building. Copper or aluminum wires are used along with appropriate cables to ensure safe and efficient power distribution.

Switches and outlets

These devices allow users to control the flow of electricity in different areas of a building. Switches are used to turn lights or appliances on and off, while outlets provide power for plugging in devices.

Circuit breakers and fuses

These are safety devices that protect electrical circuits from overloads and short circuits. Circuit breakers automatically trip to interrupt the flow of electricity when a fault occurs, while fuses melt and break the circuit in case of excessive current.

Light fixtures

These include various types of lighting fixtures such as recessed lights, ceiling lights, pendant lights, track lights, and wall sconces. They provide illumination and enhance the aesthetic appeal of the building.

Electrical panels

Also known as distribution boards or breaker boxes, electrical panels house circuit breakers or fuses and serve as a central point for controlling and distributing electrical power within a building.

Electrical conduits

These are pipes or tubes used to protect and route electrical wiring throughout the building. Conduits can be made of metal or plastic and are typically installed within walls, floors, or ceilings.

Junction boxes

These are enclosures used to protect and connect electrical wires, splices, or connections. Junction boxes provide a safe and organized environment for joining and branching electrical circuits.

Electric meters

These devices measure the amount of electricity consumed in a building. They are typically installed at the point where the electrical service enters the premises and are used for billing purposes.

Transformers

Transformers are used to step up or step down the voltage of electrical power as needed. They ensure that the right voltage is delivered to different devices and equipment within the building.

Electric motors

These devices convert electrical energy into mechanical energy and are commonly used in various building systems such as HVAC (heating, ventilation, and air conditioning) systems, elevators, pumps, and fans.

Plumbing Solution

CPVC Pipe & Fitting

CPVC (Chlorinated Polyvinyl Chloride) pipes and fittings are commonly used in plumbing systems for hot and cold water distribution. CPVC is a durable and heat-resistant material that can withstand higher temperatures compared to regular PVC pipes. It is known for its corrosion resistance and is often used in residential and commercial plumbing applications.

UPVC Pipe & Fitting

UPVC (Unplasticized Polyvinyl Chloride) pipes and fittings are widely used in plumbing systems for cold water distribution. Unlike CPVC, UPVC is not suitable for hot water applications due to its lower heat resistance. However, it is an affordable and lightweight option that offers good chemical resistance and durability.

SWR Pipe & Fitting

SWR (Soil, Waste, and Rainwater) pipes and fittings are designed for the disposal of waste, sewage, and rainwater from buildings. These pipes are typically made of PVC and are used for underground drainage systems. SWR pipes and fittings are resistant to chemicals and have a smooth inner surface to facilitate the flow of waste materials.

Special & Accessories

This category generally includes various plumbing accessories and specialized fittings that are used in specific plumbing applications. These may include items such as valves, connectors, adapters, traps, flanges, gaskets, and other components that are essential for the installation and proper functioning of plumbing systems.
